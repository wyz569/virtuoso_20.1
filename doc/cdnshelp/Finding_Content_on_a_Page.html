Finding Content on a Page


To view or locate a specific content section on the given page, click Ctrl+F to open the Find box.

It is an on-demand feature. When you search for a text globally, the results show snippets, which highlight the exact position of the searched text in the various pages of the search result. You can click the relevant page and then do Ctrl+F to go to the exact point of the occurrence of the searched text/word.

It is possible that a page appears in the search result (which is fetched because the keyword is found in its meta tag), but it does not contain that text. In this case, Ctrl+F is not effective.

We can also say that the searched keywords will be highlighted on the page. You can navigate up and down the page to look for the relevant content. Cadence Help will let you know when a searched phrase is not found by changing the background of the Find box to red.


Choose from the following actions on the Find box:


  • X: Close the find box.
  • Next and Previous arrows: Jump to the previous or next match on the page.
